What does traditional global employment look like?

Without Panther, you have to be ready to spend hundreds of dollars to employ globally. For eg., if you’re looking to hire in one country only, you’ll be spending $80,000 minimum. That’s because hiring abroad typically requires building or buying a local branch, registering it as a subsidiary of your headquarters, filing for local bank accounts, keeping up to date with changing local labor laws and collective bargaining rights, and contracting local payroll, accounting, legal, and HR personnel. This process takes months if you're lucky, a year if you're not. ‍ Panther eliminates the need for a local subsidiary by taking over the heavy lifting and saving you tens of thousands of dollars—and precious time.
